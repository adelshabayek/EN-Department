Welcome to the department of English language and literature

Faculty of Arts, Arish University

The department started its mission in 2014 with the start of the faculty of arts. There are two divisions: linguistics and translation, and English literature. We are always developing courses using technology and varying our teaching methods and strategies for a better educational service. Completing the four levels of study provides a large number of jobs: as a translator, as a teacher, and so many other language-related job opportunities.

The first year prepares you for the two future specialisations: Linguistics OR Literature

More Introduction
• First Year :

o introduction to linguistics, which is a general study of language in a scientific way, opening doors for other future courses.

o introduction to drama, poetry, and novel: where the study focuses on the elements of literary works, teaching you basic skills of understanding and criticising a carefully picked collection of literary works.... also a great opener for future courses.

o phonology: which studies the sounds of speech as a means of communication.

• By your Second year you must pick a speciality; either language & translation or English Literature

Our specialities

LANGUAGE AND TRANSLATION

• Second year focuses on more advanced levels of translation...
o you'll also study syntax, which is the arrangement of words in sentences, clauses, and phrases.
o European civilisation, which is a unique multi-disciplinary subject
• Third year :
o sociolinguistics, which is how we speak differently in varying social contexts..
o semantics, which studies the relationship between words and how we draw meaning from them
o The linguistic specialitystill considers the balance between the scientific and artistic value of language, so, courses like novel, poetry, drama, and literary appreciation continue to accompany you throughout your journey..
• Fourth year :
o contrastive analysis, which is the comparison of two language systems.
o discourse analysis, which studies language in relation to its social context.
o lexicology, which examines every feature of a word, including formation, spelling, origin, usage, and definition.. everything..
o pragmatics, focusing on conversational implicature.
o Finally, the highlight of your study as a fourth year focuses on translation theories, and a translation research project, courses that deal with the theory, description, and application of translation.
Read More

ENGLISH LITERATURE

• If you find yourself more on the artistic side, then Literature is for you.
• Second year :
o Poetry, drama, novel, and criticism, boosting your creativity and understanding of the world..
o More advanced courses include civilisation, and Greek and Roman Literature, giving you a solid ground for understanding the references of literary works and their historical background..
• Third year :
o More drama, criticism, poetry, novel and short story, offering you a wider scope of the world through art..
o Literary movements, and literary appreciation, breaking down elements of what makes a literary text a masterpiece.
o American Literature and civilisation, shedding light on the American voice in Literature and how it gained its own identity and themes, dependent from the Eurocentric, classical views.
• Fourth year :
o Shakespeare, the course is wholly dedicated to decipher Shakespeare's timeless works... one of the fathers of Elizabethan English Literature, indispensable to any English Literature student..
o the timeline is pushed further upon the study of more modern literary works, which is included in our study of Romantic poetry, Victorian and modern poetry.
o finally, the most significant area of study as a fourth year focuses on world Literature, world theatre, and comparative Literature, teaching you how to see people, ideas, and art through a bigger scope, how to analyse everything on both the micro and macro levels, how to understand everything in light of other contexts, and what makes a certain work of art timeless and relatable..
